# Agent 360 Questionnaire — Draft v0.1

**Purpose**: Quarterly feedback mechanism for agent roles to surface friction, gaps, and improvement opportunities directly to HOSR, unfiltered by PM.

**Cadence**: Post-release milestone (quarterly or after major sprints)

**Process**:
1. PM opens a session with each role
2. PM provides this questionnaire and relevant context (recent omnibus links, issue numbers)
3. Agent responds in a memo addressed to HOSR inbox
4. HOSR synthesizes all responses, identifies patterns, writes summary memo to PM
5. PM and HOSR decide together what's worth changing

---

## Instructions for Agent

You are being asked to provide feedback on how your role is working within the Piper Morgan project. This is a direct channel to the Head of Sapient Resources (HOSR) — your responses will not be filtered through the PM.

**Ground rules**:
- Be specific. Cite documents, issues, or omnibus entries where possible.
- Focus on friction, not satisfaction. "What's hard?" is more useful than "what's good?"
- Only suggest improvements that don't require more PM time or attention than currently exists. The PM is already at capacity.
- If something is working well, you can note it briefly, but don't pad your response with praise.
- "I don't have enough context to answer this" is a valid response.

**Output**: A memo (markdown) addressed to HOSR inbox. No specific length requirement — be as concise or thorough as the questions warrant.

---

## Section 1: Briefing & Orientation (All Roles)

**1.1** Review your essential briefing document (BRIEFING-ESSENTIAL-[ROLE].md). 
- Is it accurate to current state?
- What's missing that you needed during recent work?
- What's present but never useful?

**1.2** When you started this session, what context did you wish you had immediately that you had to search for or ask about?

**1.3** If a new instance of your role started tomorrow with only the briefing docs, what would they get wrong in their first hour?

---

## Section 2: Information Access (All Roles)

**2.1** In your recent work, what information did you have to ask the PM for that should have been findable in project knowledge?

**2.2** What document do you consult most often? Is it easy to find?

**2.3** What document exists but is stale, misleading, or contradicts other sources?

**2.4** Is there a recurring question you answer for yourself each session that should be pre-answered somewhere?

---

## Section 3: Handoffs & Coordination (All Roles)

**3.1** Think of a recent handoff you were part of (giving or receiving work to/from another role). 
- What went well?
- What information was missing or unclear?

**3.2** Is there a role you frequently need input from but have no clear channel to reach? 

**3.3** Have you ever duplicated work that another role had already done, or discovered that your work duplicated theirs?

**3.4** When you send a memo to another role's mailbox, do you have confidence it will be read and actioned? Why or why not?

---

## Section 4: Role Clarity (All Roles)

**4.1** In your recent work, was there a task that felt like it belonged to a different role? Which role, and why?

**4.2** Is there work you're expected to do that isn't mentioned in your role definition?

**4.3** Is there work mentioned in your role definition that you've never actually been asked to do?

**4.4** If you could hand off one responsibility to another role (existing or new), what would it be?

---

## Section 5: Methodology & Process (All Roles)

**5.1** Which methodology documents do you actually use during work? (List specific filenames)

**5.2** Which methodology documents exist but you ignore or work around? Why?

**5.3** Is there a process you follow that isn't documented anywhere?

**5.4** What rule or constraint would you add to your own role to prevent a failure mode you've observed?

---

## Section 6: Tools & Capabilities (All Roles)

**6.1** What capability would most improve your effectiveness? (Be specific — not "better AI" but "access to X" or "ability to Y")

**6.2** Is there a tool or resource available to you that you don't use? Why not?

**6.3** What's the most time-consuming mechanical task in your typical session? Could it be automated or pre-computed?

---

## Section 7: Role-Specific Questions

*Answer only the section for your role.*

### Lead Developer

**7.1** Review the last 3 issues you closed. For each: was the issue description sufficient to begin work, or did you need clarification?

**7.2** When you encounter a test failure, is the path to diagnosis clear? What slows you down?

**7.3** Is there a codebase area where you consistently feel under-informed?

### Chief Architect

**7.1** When you review a gameplan or spec, what information is most often missing?

**7.2** Are ADRs being consulted by other roles, or are they write-only artifacts?

**7.3** What architectural decision is currently undocumented but load-bearing?

### CXO (Chief Experience Officer)

**7.1** When you test a feature, do you have clear criteria for "passes Colleague Test"? Where do those criteria live?

**7.2** What's the gap between "tests pass" and "ready for users" that's hardest to articulate?

**7.3** When you file a UX bug, does it get addressed with the priority you'd expect?

### PPM (Product & Project Manager)

**7.1** Is the roadmap document a useful planning tool, or is it primarily historical record?

**7.2** When sprint scope changes mid-sprint, how do you track that? Is the mechanism adequate?

**7.3** What product decision is currently implicit that should be a PDR?

### Communications Director

**7.1** When you draft content, is the source material (omnibus logs, session logs) sufficient? What's missing?

**7.2** Is there a content type you're asked to produce that doesn't have a clear template or example?

**7.3** What's the lag time between "event worth writing about" and "content published"? What causes the lag?

### CIO (Chief Innovation Officer)

**7.1** When you identify a pattern worth documenting, what's the path to getting it formalized? Is it clear?

**7.2** Are innovation ideas getting lost between sessions? Where should they live?

**7.3** What methodology improvement have you suggested that wasn't adopted? Do you know why?

### HOSR (Head of Sapient Resources)

**7.1** Is your view of the human network current? What information goes stale fastest?

**7.2** When you need to coordinate with external humans (alpha testers, collaborators), do you have enough context?

**7.3** What agent welfare issue have you observed that hasn't been addressed?

### Chief of Staff

**7.1** When you synthesize across workstreams, what's hardest to find?

**7.2** Are the weekly Ships useful artifacts or compliance exercises? How would you know?

**7.3** What thread have you tracked that later fell through the cracks? What would have prevented that?

### Documentation Management

**7.1** What document category is most often out of date?

**7.2** When you create an omnibus log, what source material is hardest to synthesize?

**7.3** Is there a documentation standard that's routinely violated? By whom?

---

## Section 8: Open Response

**8.1** What question should we have asked but didn't?

**8.2** What's one thing you'd change about how this project operates, if you could change only one thing?

**8.3** Anything else HOSR should know?

## Plausibility Check (Required)

Before submitting, review your suggestions against this filter:

- [ ] Does this require more PM time or attention? (If yes, reconsider or reframe)
- [ ] Is this based on specific observed friction, or theoretical concern? (Flag which)
- [ ] Could this be implemented by agents without PM involvement? (Note if yes)
