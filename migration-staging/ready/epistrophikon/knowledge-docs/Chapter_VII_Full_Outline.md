## PART ONE: MORNING ROUTINE (Setup - ~1,200 words)

### Scene 1A: The Scriptorium
**Time**: Third hour (9 AM), bright spring morning
**Location**: Imperial scriptorium, somewhere in the Daphne Palace complex

**Opening Image**: Hraban at his writing desk, copying a document. Perhaps something ironic - a tax record from "Roman" territories, or a diplomatic letter referring to "barbarian" kingdoms. His withered left arm holds the parchment steady; his right hand moves with practiced grace.

**Establishing Details**:
- His daily routine (has he been doing this for ~5-6 years now?)
- His workspace - shared with other scribes, clerks, notaries
- Morning light through windows
- Sounds: scratch of styli, murmur of Greek, occasional Latin
- His position: junior clerk under Theophylact's department

**Character Mood**: Comfortable, competent, confident in his hybrid identity. He's "Korax the Knowledge-Lover" now - nickname earned by correcting a senior scholar a few years back. That story should be hinted at.

**Relationships on Display**:
- Banter with Michael the scribe (establishing him before vault scene)
- Deference to senior officials
- Easy code-switching between Greek and Latin
- Perhaps someone asks him to translate something "barbarian" (Germanic?) - he does it easily

**The Summons**:
- Theophylact appears (or sends a messenger)
- "Come with me. There's an interrogation. I need your Latin."
- Hraban's reaction: This is routine. He's done this before.
- Gathers his wax tablet (for notes), straightens his tunic
- Maybe grabs a piece of bread or fruit - assumes it'll be quick

**Key Line to Plant**: 
Theophylact: "This one's from Calabria. Speaks some Latin. Should be straightforward."
OR
Theophylact: "An interesting case. I think you'll find it educational."
(The second version hints that this is deliberate pedagogy)

## PART THREE: THE AFTERMATH (~1,800-2,500 words)

### Scene 3A: The Walk Home
**Time**: Afternoon, 16th-17th hour (4-5 PM)
**Location**: Through Constantinople streets
**Already Drafted**: Final paragraphs of current draft provide good start

**Extend This Section**:

Hraban emerges into late afternoon light, disoriented. He assumes he should return to the scriptorium, but he's hours late. Does he go back? Or does he just... walk?

**Option A - He Returns to Work**:
- Tries to resume copying
- Can't focus
- Michael asks "What happened down there?"
- Hraban can't explain
- Leaves early (unprecedented)

**Option B - He Wanders** (Stronger choice):
- Doesn't even consider going back to work
- Walks without destination
- The city he thought he knew looks different

**Sensory Overwhelm**:
- Every voice he hears: which language? Which dialect?
- Every person: Roman? Greek? Arab? Armenian? How can he tell?
- Architecture: layers of history visible suddenly
- The Theodosian walls in the distance: which Rome built those?

**Specific Encounters** (2-3 brief vignettes):

1. **The Bread Vendor** (callback):
   - Hraban buys bread from the vendor whose cry interrupted interrogation
   - Vendor speaks Greek, calls bread "Roman bread"
   - Hraban asks: "Where is your family from?"
   - Vendor: "Antioch, three generations back. Why?"
   - Hraban: "Nothing. Just curious."
   - Realizes: this man never questions his identity

2. **The Purple Shop**:
   - Passes a silk merchant
   - Purple cloth in window
   - Remembers the texture
   - Merchant (in Greek): "You like? Only for citizens, you understand."
   - Hraban: "I understand." (But does he?)

3. **The Latin Church** (if there is one):
   - Hears Latin hymn
   - Sounds foreign to his ears now
   - Or: sounds like home but HOME feels foreign
   - Doesn't go in

4. **Fellow Clerks**:
   - Runs into colleagues from scriptorium
   - They speak Greek, assume he'll join them for wine
   - He makes excuse, continues walking
   - They call after him in Greek: "Korax! What's wrong?"
   - The nickname sounds different now

## CHAPTER THEMES & MOTIFS

**The Palimpsest**:
- Vault's carved dates from old empire
- Constantinople built on older cities
- Hraban's identity layered over earlier selves
- New text written over scraped parchment

**Counting**:
- Hraban's coping mechanism
- Mathematical certainty when identity uncertain
- The language he counts in = the language he thinks in
- Progression: starts counting in Latin, ends in Greek

**The Withered Arm**:
- Physical marker of difference
- Empire's "withered" West
- But what withers can also heal/scar/strengthen differently
- His disability becomes metaphor but never ONLY metaphor

**Light and Darkness**:
- Bright morning scriptorium
- Dark vault (crisis)
- Evening streets (confusion)
- Night vigil (questioning)
- Dawn (uncertain beginning)

**Languages as Clothing**:
- He puts on and takes off languages
- But which is nakedness? Which is disguise?
- By chapter end: all feel like costumes OR all feel like skin
