## 900-949: FOUNDATION PERIOD

### 913: Symeon of Bulgaria's Imperial Ambitions
**Event**: Symeon crowned "Emperor of the Bulgarians and Romans"
**Context**: Bulgarian challenge to Roman imperial monopoly
**Roman Response**: Diplomatic and military resistance
**Relevance**: Establishes ongoing question of "who is Roman?"

### 920s-940s: Constantine VII Porphyrogennetos's Education
**Event**: Future emperor receives comprehensive classical education
**Context**: Scholarly emperor-to-be immersed in Roman learning
**Impact**: Will shape his reign (945-959) as patron of arts, learning, diplomacy
**Relevance to Novel**: Creates the intellectual environment Hraban enters (949)

### 946-949: Liutprand's First Period at Berengar II's Court
**Event**: Liutprand serves Berengar II of Italy
**Context**: Before defection to Otto I
**Documented**: Liutprand had "wonderful voice" during this period
**Relevance**: Establishes Liutprand's beautiful voice before 949 embassy

## 968: LIUTPRAND'S RETURN & PURPLE SILK HUMILIATION

### June 4, 968: Liutprand Arrives as Otto's Ambassador
**Context**: 19 years after bringing Hraban (age 30 now)
**Status**: Bishop, senior diplomat, Otto II's ambassador
**Mission**: Negotiate marriage alliance for Otto II
**Reception**: Immediate humiliation

**Liutprand's Condition**:
- Age 47, voice ruined
- Historical: Fell "seriously ill from boundless depression" during mission
- Once-beautiful voice now hoarse, strained, music gone
- Embodied failure matching diplomatic failure

**KALDELLIS CONTEXT**: Liutprand's *Relatio de legatione Constantinopolitana* is notoriously bitter and unreliable. Written as revenge narrative after humiliation. Modern scholars must read it critically, not as objective account.

**Women Present**:
- Adelaide of Italy context (Otto's powerful empress, future enemy of Theophanu)
- German noblewomen in broader diplomatic context

**Event**: Diplomatic confrontation over imperial legitimacy

### July 968: Liutprand Departs
**Context**: Mission complete failure, returns to Otto bitter

**Event**: Emperor murdered in palace bedroom

**Theophano's Role**:

**Theophano's Calculation**:
- Expected to marry Tzimiskes (planned alliance)

- Betrayed by co-conspirator who chooses throne over her

**Hraban's Position**:

- **Consensus vs. Debate**: Noted where scholarly disagreement exists
