## NARRATIVE NOTES

### Possible Structural Approaches

**Option A - Linear with Flashbacks**:
- Start with Ch I (1949), proceed chronologically
- Chapter VI and VII can be placed where they naturally fit in emotional arc

**Option B - Frame Narrative**:
- Open with old Hraban (Epilogue scene)
- "It began with mechanical birds..." 
- Flash back to full story
- Return to frame periodically

**Option C - In Medias Res**:
- Open with Chapter VII (the crisis)
- Flash back to explain how he got there
- Then proceed forward from crisis

**Current Decision**: Linear chronological for first draft (Option A)

### Thematic Through-Lines

1. **The Body as Metaphor**: Hraban's withered arm = Western Empire's "weakness"
2. **Language as Magic**: Words shape reality, spells change worlds
3. **Theater of Power**: Performance isn't lying; it's how power works
4. **Belonging as Choice**: Identity is active, not passive
5. **The Palimpsest**: Everything built on something earlier (vault, empire, person)
