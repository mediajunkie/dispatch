# Hraban/Korax - Main Character Dossier
*The Epistrophikon/Anazosis - Working Character Profile*

## Basic Information
**Birth Name**: Hraban ("raven")  
**Birth Year**: ~937-938 CE  
**Origin**: Saxon/Frankish borderlands  
**Physical Distinction**: Withered left arm from birth  

## Name Evolution
1. **949-952**: "Hraban" / "ὁ Φράγκος" (the Frank)
2. **952-958**: "Hrabanus Interpres" / "Κόραξ ὁ Φιλομαθής" (Korax the Knowledge-Lover)
3. **958-965**: "Corvus" / "Κόραξ ὁ Διγλωσσος" (Korax the Bilingual)
4. **965-972**: "Eugenios ho Korax" / "Εὐγένιος ὁ Ῥωμαῖος" (Eugene the Roman)
5. **Post-972**: "Hrabanus Graecus" / "der griechische Rabe" (the Greek Raven)

## Origin Story
- Third son of minor Saxon nobility near Magdeburg
- Named after Rabanus Maurus, but uses Germanic form
- Withered arm interpreted as "God's mark" - unsuitable for warfare, destined for wisdom
- Family serves various Frankish/Saxon courts; grandfather died fighting Vikings
- Educated at Fulda Abbey - Irish monks notice his exceptional memory
- Uncle Adalbert (cathedral canon at Mainz) arranges position with Berengar's embassy
- **Mother's gift**: Small bronze/silver fibula (cloak pin) with Germanic runic markings overcarved with a cross - his physical talisman, kept in his left hand for focus

## Physical Description
- Dark hair earning him the "raven" nickname
- Slight build, compensating for arm with exceptional dexterity in right hand
- Intense dark eyes - "watching like his namesake"
- **At 17-18**: Coltish, just shedding baby fat, not quite a man yet, minimal facial hair compared to Greek/Armenian peers
- Develops Byzantine fashion: longer hair, silk preferences (when he can afford it), use of perfumes
- Withered left arm: shortened, limited mobility, but he learns to write holding parchment with it
- **Typical dress**: Simple chiton (tunic) and chlamys (cloak) in undyed or earth tones (brown, dark green, dark blue), leather belt, simple shoes - owns 2-3 outfits total

## Personality Traits
- Observant to the point of unsettling others
- Exceptional memory - can recite texts after single reading
- Dry wit emerges as he grows comfortable
- Fascinated by patterns, etymologies, connections
- Initially defensive about disability, later sees it as advantage
- Develops Byzantine sophistication while maintaining Germanic directness

## Linguistic Journey
- **Native**: East Frankish dialect (Theodiscus)
- **Educated**: Church Latin, basic Greek
- **949-955**: Develops diplomatic Greek, notices Latin loanwords
- **955-965**: Masters literary Greek, begins thinking in it
- **965-972**: Speaks Greek with slight philosophical accent, Latin with Greek syntax
- **Post-972**: German sounds foreign to his own ears

## Key Relationships
- **Theophylact the Logothete**: First Byzantine mentor, teaches him court navigation - Hraban lives in his household near the palace
- **Constantine VII**: Brief but formative encounters about scholarship
- **Nikephoros Phokas**: Sees past his disability, values his intelligence
- **Brother Domenico**: Calabrian prisoner who challenges his worldview
- **Basil II**: Corresponds with him in later years
- **Theophanu**: Complex relationship - met when both young in palace, she took him for kitchen boy, he thought she was pretending to be princess; now (955) he pines for her but she regards him as brother
- **Daily associates (955)**:
  - **Michael the Scribe**: Older, good-hearted, career scribe, no ambition
  - **Ioannes**: Constantinople third son from good but not wealthy family, sophisticated, knows the city, teases Hraban about his walks and love life
  - **Andreas**: Armenian-Greek heritage, family been Greek-speaking clerks for generations, fellow code-switcher and natural ally

## Daily Life in 955 CE (Age 17-18)

**Living Situation**: 
- Resides in Theophylact's household near the Great Palace (Chalke Gate area)
- Small private room (formerly storage/servant quarters) with window overlooking courtyard
- Part of Theophylact's *officium* (administrative household)

**Position & Title**:
- Functionally: apprentice clerk in Theophylact's *skrinion* (office/department)
- Others call him: "Korax," "the Frank," "the Logothete's boy"
- Possibly *hypographeus* (under-secretary) but title vague and uncertain
- Functions: copying, translating, authenticating documents, occasional interpreting
- He's unclear on his exact status - servant? familia? court functionary?

**Daily Schedule**:
- **Dawn-Third Hour**: Morning prayers, breakfast (bread, watered wine, cheese), reports to scriptorium/library
- **Third-Ninth Hour**: Main work - copying manuscripts, translating documents, authentication tasks (like forgery detection)
- **Afternoon**: Finish work, sometimes observes palace ceremonies, takes long walks through city
- **Evening**: Studies Greek, reads, practices languages, dinner with other clerks, early to bed

**Current Task (Day of Interrogation)**: Comparing two manuscripts to determine which is authentic for monastery near Mount Athos claiming tax exemption

## Motivations & Arc
- **Initial**: Prove himself despite disability, advance family interests
- **Middle**: Intellectual fascination with Byzantine civilization
- **Later**: Genuine conversion to Roman identity, sense of historical mission
- **Final**: Preserve and transmit understanding between worlds

## Unique Abilities
- Can write in multiple scripts simultaneously (party trick)
- Remembers conversations verbatim years later
- Sees patterns in language others miss
- Uses disability to disarm opponents - they underestimate him
- Develops network of information through other "overlooked" people

## Defining Moments
1. **949**: First sight of Constantinople - realizes his provincial origins
2. **952**: Earns "Philomathēs" by correcting senior scholar
3. **955**: Calabrian prisoner scene - identity crisis begins
4. **963**: Witnesses Nikephoros's coup - understands Byzantine power
5. **969**: Survives palace coup by being useful to all sides
6. **972**: Departs with Theophanu - realizes he's become what he once mocked

## Internal Conflicts
- Germanic pride vs. Byzantine sophistication
- Christian duty vs. intellectual honesty
- Loyalty to origins vs. adopted identity
- Physical limitation vs. mental ambition
- Observer vs. participant in history

## Writing Notes
- Use raven imagery throughout - watching, collecting shiny things (knowledge), messenger between worlds
- Withered arm as metaphor for empires - what seems weak may be differently strong
- His linguistic observations mirror reader's journey understanding Byzantine = Roman
- Comic moments from cultural confusion balance heavier themes
- His fascination with patterns/connections appeals to modern puzzle-loving readers
