## II. THE LIUTPRAND FACTOR - RICHEST MATERIAL YET

### What the Dossier Gives Us

**1. The "Personae Not Personality" Insight**
This is GOLD. Liutprand understood written self-invention. He code-switched, performed different versions of himself for different audiences. This makes him the **anti-teacher** for Hraban:
- Liutprand shows that performance IS power
- But 968 shows what happens when you can't control which persona projects
- Hraban learns to BECOME his performance, not just perform it

**2. The Voice Detail**
Liutprand launched his career with his "wonderful voice" as child singer. This is **embodied talent** that gave him access.
- Contrast: Hraban's withered arm is embodied limitation
- But both use their bodies to navigate power
- This could be a beautiful scene: young Hraban hearing Liutprand's beautiful voice,  realizing voices and bodies are currencies

**3. The Four Eunuchs**
949 embassy brought "four de luxe eunuchs" as gifts - "fully castrated, the premium variety."
- What happened to them?
- Did young Hraban interact with them?
- Early moment where Hraban encounters human cost of diplomacy

Berengar's "notorious parsimony" meant Liutprand brought inadequate gifts in 949.
- Diplomatic embarrassment arriving at wealthiest court with poor gifts
- How does 11-year-old Hraban experience this humiliation?
- Teaches him about gap between Western and Eastern resources
- Foreshadows purple silk incident (the gift that can't be given)

**5. The 955 Defection**
Liutprand flees to Otto in 955 - **THE SAME YEAR AS THE VAULT SCENE**.
- Does Hraban learn of it? Feel abandoned? Betrayed?
- Or understand it as political necessity?
- This could be why Theophylact stages the vault interrogation - testing Hraban's loyalty when his patron has defected

**6. The Bilingual Showing-Off**
Liutprand wrote Greek in Greek letters, then transliteration, then Latin translation - peacocking his linguistic access.
- Perfect model for Hraban's language acquisition scenes
- But Hraban will surpass him - truly bilingual vs. performatively bilingual
- Could create scene where Hraban corrects Liutprand's Greek (subtle power shift)
