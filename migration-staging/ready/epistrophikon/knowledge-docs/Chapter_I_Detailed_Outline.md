1. **Introduce Hraban** - personality, disability, intelligence, ambition

## PART ONE: DEPARTURE FROM BERENGAR'S COURT (~1,500 words)

### Scene 1A: The Commission
**Location**: King Berengar II's palace, Pavia
**Time**: Early morning, departure day

**Opening Image**: NOT Hraban. Open with Liutprand - confident, experienced diplomat, packing for his second embassy to Constantinople. Show him from outside before we meet our protagonist.

**Liutprand's Attitude**:
- This is his victory lap - Berengar is sending him to the "Greeks" again
- He's bringing gifts for Constantine VII
- Expects to be received with honor (as he was in first embassy)
- Perhaps a touch of condescension toward "Eastern Rome"

**Enter Hraban**:
- Not as embassy member initially
- Perhaps brought by uncle/patron: "My nephew is learned beyond his years..."
- Liutprand's assessment: Young, eager, marked by God (withered arm)
- Liutprand's decision: "He can carry scrolls, at least. Keep records."
- Hraban's reaction: This is his CHANCE

**Hraban's Stakes**:
- Third son, no inheritance
- Withered arm makes him unsuitable for knighthood
- Brilliant mind but provincial education
- This embassy = his only path to significance
- Desperate to prove himself to Liutprand

**Relationships Established**:
- Liutprand: Patron, but distant, conditional approval
- Uncle/sponsor: Pride and worry
- Other embassy members: Mix of curiosity and doubt about the "crippled boy"

**The World He's Leaving**:
- Lombard Italy (Christianized barbarian kingdom)
- Latin as language of learning
- Stories about Rome (the OLD Rome, the ruins)
- "Byzantine" = "Greek" = effeminate, scheming, decadent
- But also: rich, sophisticated, mysterious

**His Expectations**:
- He'll see wonders
- He'll learn Greek (language of the New Testament!)
- He'll meet the Emperor
- He'll return home impressive and Important
- Maybe earn a position in a cathedral, become a bishop someday

**Key Line to Plant**:
Liutprand: "Remember, boy - they call themselves Romans, but they're Greeks. Don't let the marble fool you."
Hraban: "Yes, dominus." (But inwardly: How can they be Romans if they speak Greek?)

## PART TWO: FIRST SIGHT OF CONSTANTINOPLE (~1,200 words)

### Scene 2A: Approach by Sea
**Location**: Ship approaching Constantinople, early morning
**Time**: Dawn

**The Moment**:
This needs to be SPECTACULAR. Every Byzantine source talks about the impact of seeing the City from the sea. This is Hraban's "Wizard of Oz" moment - leaving Kansas.

**Sensory Overload**:
- Dawn light on the Golden Horn
- The Theodosian Walls - massive, ancient, ROMAN
- Dome of Hagia Sophia catching first light
- The Great Palace complex spreading across terraces
- Ships from everywhere - Arab dhows, Rus longships, Italian merchants
- Languages mixing on the docks
- Smell of the city - spices, fish, humanity

**Hraban's Reaction**:
- Speechless
- Physically overwhelmed (nearly drops something?)
- His first thought IN GREEK (does he realize?)
- Tries to count the domes, the towers, the ships - too many
- Realizes: Pavia could fit in one district

**Liutprand's Reaction**:
- Seen it before, less impressed
- But: "Impressive, isn't it? They spend their wealth on appearance."
- Already framing it as superficial

**Landing**:
- Byzantine harbor officials (efficient, bureaucratic)
- Documents checked, tribute recorded
- Assigned lodgings (good ones - Berengar is on good terms)
- First taste of Byzantine organization

**Key Moment**:
Byzantine official (in accented Latin): "Welcome to New Rome."
Hraban thinks: This IS Rome. The real one.
But doesn't say it.

---

### Scene 2B: Into the City
**Location**: Journey from harbor to their lodgings
**Length**: ~700 words

**Purpose**:
- Show Constantinople's diversity and organization
- Hraban's sensory overload continues
- Establish the city as character

**The Mese (Main Street)**:
- Colonnaded boulevard
- Statues of emperors and heroes
- Forums at intervals
- Street vendors calling in Greek, Armenian, Arabic
- Hraban's head swiveling

**Details Hraban Notices** (showing his observant nature):
- Latin inscriptions on old monuments (Roman continuity!)
- Greek everywhere in speech
- Aqueducts functioning (unlike Rome!)
- Drainage systems, clean streets
- Mixed population - can't tell "Roman" from "foreigner"

**A Telling Encounter**:
- They stop at a fountain
- Hraban stares at a bronze statue
- Old man (in Greek) offers to tell the statue's story
- Hraban doesn't understand
- Embassy translator interprets
- Hraban feels his ignorance keenly

**Arrival at Lodgings**:
- A decent house (not palace, not hovel)
- Byzantine servant (speaks Latin) shows them rooms
- Hraban's space: Smaller than others (his status), but better than any room he's had
- Window overlooks the city
- He stands there, staring, until Liutprand calls him

## PART THREE: THE PREPARATION (~1,000 words)

### Scene 3A: Waiting and Learning
**Location**: Their lodgings
**Time**: Days/weeks (compress)

**The Wait**:
- Byzantine protocol: They must wait for imperial summons
- Could be days, could be weeks
- Liutprand: Annoyed but expected
- Hraban: Uses time to learn

**Hraban's Activities**:
- Finds a tutor (Greek-speaking priest? Patient servant?)
- Begins intensive Greek study
- Explores neighborhood (with minder)
- Sketches the city (if he draws?)
- Copies Greek inscriptions, tries to translate
- Asks constant questions

**Culture Shock Moments**:
1. **Food**: Olive oil everywhere, garum on everything, no butter
   - Hraban's reaction: Initially repulsed, then acquired taste?
   
2. **Baths**: Public bathhouses still functioning
   - Germanic modesty vs. Roman casualness
   - Hraban's disability on display
   - But: No one seems to care much

3. **Religion**: Orthodox practices different from Latin
   - Icons everywhere
   - Different liturgy
   - But: Still Christian, still familiar enough

4. **Class**: Eunuchs in positions of power
   - This confuses/disturbs the Lombards
   - Hraban: Curious despite himself

**Growing Divide**:
- Other embassy members: Homesick, critical
- "These Greeks and their perfumes!"
- "Real men don't mince about like that."
- Hraban: Quieter, observing, less judgmental

**Liutprand Notices**:
- "Don't go native on me, boy."
- But also: "Your Greek is improving. Good."

---

### Scene 3B: The Summons
**Location**: Their lodgings
**Time**: Morning

**The Moment**:
- Imperial messenger arrives (impressive uniform, ceremony)
- Announces (in Greek, then Latin): Audience tomorrow
- Liutprand's triumph
- Hraban: Terror and excitement

**Preparation**:
- Best clothes
- Rehearsal of protocol
- Gifts prepared (what did Berengar send?)
- Liutprand coaching: Posture, etiquette, never turn back on Emperor
- Hraban's role: Carry scrolls, stay quiet, watch and learn

**The Night Before**:
- Hraban can't sleep
- Reviews Greek phrases
- Imagines the throne room
- His withered arm aches (nerves?)
- Thinks of home - will they be proud?
- Thinks of future - this could change everything

## PART FOUR: THE MAGNAURA (~2,500-3,000 words)

### Scene 4A: The Approach
**Location**: Journey to Palace
**Time**: Morning

**Processional Quality**:
- They're escorted by imperial guards
- Through increasingly grand palace courtyards
- Each gate, each corridor more impressive
- Building anticipation

**Hraban's Observations**:
- Mosaics depicting imperial victories
- Every surface rich with color
- Guards in ceremonial armor
- Fountains, gardens, marble everywhere
- He's counting details (coping mechanism)

**The Wait**:
- Antechamber outside throne room
- Other foreign envoys waiting
- Rus? Arabs? Armenians?
- Hierarchy visible in where each group waits
- Liutprand positioned respectfully (Berengar has status)

**Tension**:
- Hraban needs to piss (boy's bladder)
- Too afraid to ask
- Shifting weight
- Liutprand notices: "Hold it together."

### Scene 4B: The Throne Room - CENTERPIECE SCENE
**Location**: Magnaura Palace throne room
**Time**: Tenth hour (noon)

**THIS IS THE SCENE - Needs most detail and care**

**Entry**:
- Massive doors open
- Sound of organ music
- Curtains part
- The throne room revealed

**The Space**:
- Vast hall (give dimensions that matter to an 11-year-old)
- Gold everywhere, catching light
- Mosaics on ceiling - Christ Pantocrator
- The mechanical tree - bronze and gold, filled with birds
- The mechanical lions flanking the throne
- The throne itself - gold, elevated

**The Performance Begins**:
1. **The Lions Roar**:
   - Hydraulic systems activate
   - Bronze jaws open
   - Roaring sound (pipes? machinery?)
   - Tails sweeping floor
   - Hraban's reaction: Fear then wonder

2. **The Birds Sing**:
   - Different species, different songs
   - Actually mechanical (not real birds)
   - How is this possible?
   - Even Liutprand impressed (barely admits it)

3. **The Throne Rises**:
   - Emperor Constantine VII seated
   - Machinery activates
   - Throne elevates toward ceiling
   - Emperor changes vestments during elevation
   - Reappears in different robes (purple, gold)
   - Descends again

**Constantine VII**:
- First description: The Emperor as theater
- Then: The man behind the costume
- Perhaps 40 years old, scholarly bearing
- His voice (when he speaks): Cultured, measured
- His Greek: Pure, Atticizing

**The Audience**:
- Liutprand presents gifts
- Speaks in Latin (translator interprets)
- Constantine responds in Greek
- Diplomatic exchange (compliments, assurances)

**Hraban's Role**:
- Stands holding scrolls
- Trying not to stare
- Failing
- At some point, Constantine's eyes land on him
- A moment of recognition? Curiosity about the young Lombard?
- Constantine says something in Greek
- Translator: "His majesty notes your... marked servant."
- Liutprand (stiffening): "My clerk, majesty. Useful despite his... affliction."
- Constantine: (in Latin!) "God marks those He will use for wisdom."
- Hraban: Floored that the Emperor speaks Latin
- And that he's noticed him at all

**The Dismissal**:
- More ceremony
- Backing out of throne room (Hraban nearly trips)
- Doors close
- Silence of antechamber
- Then: Explosion of reaction from embassy

---

### Scene 4C: The Aftermath
**Location**: Returning to lodgings
**Length**: ~500 words

**Reactions**:
- Liutprand: Satisfied but dismissive
  - "Theater. All theater. They spend on show what they should spend on swords."
  - But Hraban noticed: He was impressed too
  
- Other embassy members: Mix of awe and scorn
  - "Mechanical tricks! Like mountebanks!"
  - "But imagine the craftsmen who built that..."
  
- Hraban: Silent, processing

**That Night**:
- Hraban alone in his room
- Can't stop thinking about it
- The birds, the throne, the emperor's eyes
- The Latin phrase: "God marks those He will use for wisdom"
- Touches his withered arm
- For first time, thinks: Maybe this isn't a curse
- Maybe it's a sign

## PART FIVE: THE DECISION SEED (~800 words)

### Scene 5: The Dinner
**Location**: Constantine VII's private dinner (smaller affair)
**Time**: Evening, same day or next

**The Invitation**:
- Unexpected honor
- Liutprand invited to dine with emperor (small group)
- Hraban: Allowed to attend (as curiosity? as reward?)

**The Meal**:
- Much less formal than throne room
- Constantine: Intellectual, engaging, asks questions
- Discussion: Philosophy, theology, books, maps
- Liutprand: Holding his own, impressive
- Hraban: Listening, absorbing

**Constantine's Attention**:
- At some point, Constantine asks Hraban (in Latin):
  - "You copy well. Have you studied in monasteries?"
  - Hraban's answer (nervous but clear)
  - Constantine: Interested, asks more questions
  - About his education, his reading, his ambitions

**The Offer** (Subtle, Not Explicit):
- Constantine to Liutprand: "I have many texts to copy. Latin, Greek. Translations needed."
- "If you know of qualified copyists..."
- Looks at Hraban
- Doesn't SAY "Leave the boy"
- But the implication

**Liutprand's Response**:
- Non-committal
- "We must return to King Berengar..."
- But also not refusing
- Political calculation visible

**Hraban's Reaction**:
- Doesn't understand fully yet
- But feels: Something is being offered
- A door is opening
- He could stay here
- He could learn here
- He could be MORE than third son of minor noble
