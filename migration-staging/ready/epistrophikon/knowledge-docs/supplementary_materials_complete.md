## 1. THEMATIC REPOSITORY

### The Central Question: "What Makes a Roman?"
**Evolution Throughout Novel:**
- **Act I**: Question encountered but not understood (mechanical acceptance)
- **Act II**: Question posed philosophically, cannot be answered (vault scene crisis)
- **Act III**: Question answered through living, not philosophy

**The Answer (Discovered, Not Stated):**
- Rome is choosing Rome until choice becomes nature
- Rome is surviving Rome (violence, beauty, betrayal, continuity)
- Rome is performance believed until it becomes truth
- Rome is transformation that never completes
- Rome is the story you tell yourself until you believe it

**Key Moments:**
- [CF: 3.2.2] Constantine first poses question (age 14)
- [CF: 7.2.3] Domenico interrogates, Hraban cannot answer (age 17)
- [CF: 9.1.2] Theophano: "We're both performing"
- [CF: 13.1.3] Finally understood through survival
- [CF: 16.4.1] Living as "The Greek" - full answer

## 2. CHARACTER VOICE NOTES

### Hraban/Korax/Eugenios/"The Greek"
**Age 11-17 (Act I):**
- Internal voice: Wondering, overwhelmed, counting for comfort
- Speech: Hesitant Greek, formal Latin, occasional Germanic
- Characteristic: Questions everything internally, says little externally

**Age 18-31 (Act II):**
- Internal voice: Analytical, comparing, surviving
- Speech: Fluent Greek, maintained Latin, forgotten Germanic
- Characteristic: Translates constantly, even when not translating

**Age 31-34 (Act III):**
- Internal voice: Accepting, ironic, understanding
- Speech: All languages fluid, choosing which to use strategically
- Characteristic: Sees everything as translation, including himself

**Verbal Habits:**
- Counts when stressed (steps, stones, breaths)
- Thinks in questions
- Makes connections between disparate things
- Never entirely certain

---

### Constantine VII Porphyrogennetos
- **Voice**: Scholarly, indirect, teaching through questions
- **Speech Pattern**: Literary Greek, historical references, botanical metaphors
- **Key Phrases**: "What makes a Roman?" "Keep thinking"
- **Characteristic**: Purple ink, soil-stained fingers, books over swords

---

### Theophano the Elder
- **Voice**: Sharp intelligence, calculated warmth, strategic
- **Speech Pattern**: Palace Greek with Laconian edges when emotional
- **Key Phrases**: "We're both performing" "Remember where you came from"
- **Characteristic**: Every word chosen, nothing accidental

---

### Nikephoros II Phokas
- **Voice**: Military directness, theological severity
- **Speech Pattern**: Commands not conversations, ascetic imagery
- **Key Phrases**: "Where was Otto when we defended civilization?"
- **Characteristic**: Contradictions embodied - monk-soldier

---

### John I Tzimiskes
- **Voice**: Pragmatic, flexible, less severe than Nikephoros
- **Speech Pattern**: Military but diplomatic
- **Key Phrases**: "You're useful" "Empires need hollow men"
- **Characteristic**: Understands necessity over principle

---

### Liutprand of Cremona
**949 (Beautiful Voice):**
- Musical Latin, confident, dismissive of provincial
- Voice like water through stone

**968 (Ruined Voice):**
- Hoarse, bitter, broken music
- Same words but different instrument

---

### Abu Firas al-Hamdani
- **Voice**: Poetic even in prose, philosophical, accepting
- **Speech Pattern**: Arabic musicality in Greek, metaphorical
- **Key Phrases**: "Poetry is the register of the Arabs" "Neither of us believes in victory"
- **Characteristic**: Finds beauty in captivity

---

### Brother Domenico
- **Voice**: Socratic, challenging, never satisfied with answers
- **Speech Pattern**: Calabrian Greek, theological precision
- **Key Phrases**: "What makes a Roman?" "Show me your Romanness"
- **Characteristic**: Questions as weapons

---

### Theophanu
- **Voice**: Educated precision, young but never naive
- **Speech Pattern**: Perfect palace Greek, learning German
- **Key Phrases**: "I'm going as Rome itself" "We're both servants to impossibility"
- **Characteristic**: Understands her role completely

---

### Basil the Eunuch
- **Voice**: Gentle pragmatism, survivor's wisdom
- **Speech Pattern**: Soft, considered, insider knowledge
- **Key Phrases**: "We're necessary contradictions" "Some things are more permanent"
- **Characteristic**: Sees everything, judges little

---

### Zoe (Alexandros's Daughter)
**Age 10-15:**
- Direct child's honesty

**Age 25-27:**
- Competent authority

---

---

### Alexandros (Tavern Keeper)

## 6. WRITING REMINDERS

### Techniques to Maintain

**Sensory Grounding:**
- Every scene needs minimum 3 senses
- Smells especially evocative for memory
- Temperature/texture for immediacy

**Language Consciousness:**
- Which language is Hraban thinking in?
- Translation errors/impossibilities
- Code-switching as character revelation

**Counting Habit:**
- When stressed, Hraban counts
- Numbers as ordering mechanism
- Mathematical precision vs. emotional chaos

**Women Present/Absent:**
- Every scene notes women (or their absence)
- Different spaces (male/female/mixed)
- Female networks of information

**Historical Accuracy:**
- Use "Roman" not "Byzantine"
- Greek East, Latin West
- Specific dates/events anchored

---

### What to Avoid

**Anachronisms:**
- Byzantine is 19th-century term
- Medieval mindset, not modern psychology
- No nationalism (empire not nation)

**Over-Explaining:**
- Trust reader to understand parallels
- Let themes emerge through action
- Avoid stating the metaphor

**Clean Resolution:**
- Identity remains complicated
- Translation never completes
- Questions generate questions

**Modern Sensibilities:**
- Age gaps were different (Theophanu marriage)
- Violence was governance
- Religious thought was political
