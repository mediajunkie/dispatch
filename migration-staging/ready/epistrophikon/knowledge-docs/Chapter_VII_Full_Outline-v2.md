### Scene 1A: The Scriptorium
**Time**: Third hour (9 AM), bright spring morning
**Location**: Imperial library/scriptorium where Hraban works with other clerks

**Opening Image**: Hraban at work table comparing two manuscripts side by side - authentication task for Theophylact. A monastery near Mount Athos claims tax exemption; one document is likely forged. His withered left arm holds one parchment steady while his right hand traces text, making notes on wax tablet.

**Establishing Details**:
- His daily routine (he's been doing this work for ~5-6 years now)
- Shared workspace with Ioannes, Andreas, and Michael

- Sounds: scratch of styli, murmur of Greek, occasional Latin

**Character Mood**: Comfortable, competent, confident in his hybrid identity. He's "Korax the Knowledge-Lover" now - earned by correcting a senior scholar. Focused on the forgery task - he's supposed to report findings this afternoon.

**Relationships on Display**:
- **Ioannes and Andreas idling**, talking about girls, teasing Hraban about his mysterious walks
- "Where do you disappear to, Korax? Meeting someone?"
- Teasing about women's reactions to his arm (he deflects with humor)
- **Andreas watching with understanding** (fellow outsider)
- **Michael working diligently**, ignoring the banter
- Hraban trying to concentrate despite distractions

**The Summons**:
- Theophylact appears (or sends a messenger)

- Hraban's reaction: Routine. He's done this before. But also - the forgery task!
- Brief anxiety about leaving work unfinished

**Extend This Section**:

Hraban emerges into late afternoon light, disoriented. He assumes he should return to the scriptorium, but he's hours late. Does he go back? Or does he just... walk?

**Option A - He Returns to Work**:

## CHAPTER THEMES & MOTIFS

**The Palimpsest**:
- Vault's carved dates from old empire
- Constantinople built on older cities
- Hraban's identity layered over earlier selves
- New text written over scraped parchment

**Counting**:
- Hraban's coping mechanism
- Mathematical certainty when identity uncertain
- The language he counts in = the language he thinks in
- Progression: starts counting in Latin, ends in Greek

**The Withered Arm**:
- Physical marker of difference
- Empire's "withered" West
- But what withers can also heal/scar/strengthen differently
- His disability becomes metaphor but never ONLY metaphor

**Light and Darkness**:
- Bright morning scriptorium
- Dark vault (crisis)
- Evening streets (confusion)
- Night vigil (questioning)
- Dawn (uncertain beginning)

**Languages as Clothing**:
- He puts on and takes off languages
- But which is nakedness? Which is disguise?
- By chapter end: all feel like costumes OR all feel like skin
